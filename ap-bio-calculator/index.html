üìö AP Biology Scoring Guide

Exam Structure

  • Section I: 60 Multiple Choice (90 minutes)
  • Section II: 6 Free Response (90 minutes)
  • Total Time: 3 hours
  • Scoring: Each section = 50% of final score

Score Interpretation

  • 5 (Extremely Qualified): 8-10% of students
  • 4 (Well Qualified): 20-22% of students
  • 3 (Qualified): 35-37% of students
  • 2 (Possibly Qualified): 21-23% of students
  • 1 (No Recommendation): 18-20% of students

College Credit

  • Most colleges accept scores of 3 or higher
  • Top-tier schools may require a 4 or 5
  • Credit granted: typically 4-8 semester hours
  • Check with your target colleges for specific policies

Composite Score Ranges

  • Score 5: 72-120 composite
  • Score 4: 58-71 composite
  • Score 3: 43-57 composite
  • Score 2: 28-42 composite
  • Score 1: 0-27 composite

Note: These ranges are approximate and may vary slightly by year.

How accurate is this AP Bio score calculator?

Our calculator uses the official College Board scoring guidelines and conversion tables. While we strive for accuracy, remember that actual AP scores can vary slightly due to curve adjustments each year. This calculator provides a reliable estimate within ¬±0.5 points.

Is there a penalty for wrong answers on the multiple choice?

No! Since 2011, the College Board eliminated the guessing penalty. You only earn points for correct answers, so it's always better to guess than leave a question blank.

How are Free Response Questions (FRQs) scored?

FRQs are scored by trained AP readers using detailed rubrics:
‚Ä¢ Long FRQs (Questions 1-2): 8-10 points each

What's a good AP Biology score?

Generally, a score of 3 or higher is considered "passing" and qualifies for college credit at most institutions. A score of 4 or 5 is excellent and accepted by virtually all colleges, including Ivy League schools.

When do AP Biology scores come out?

Official AP scores are typically released in early to mid-July. Students can access their scores online through their College Board account.

Can I use this calculator during the exam?

No, calculators are not permitted during the AP Biology exam. However, you can use this tool after the exam to estimate your score, or during practice tests to track your progress.
